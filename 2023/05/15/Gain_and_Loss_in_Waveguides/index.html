How To Measure Gain

The spectrum of optical gain GGG in waveguides can be obtained by measuring the spontaneous emission spectrum modulated by Fabry-perot cavity.

G(λ)=(1/L)ln⁡(1/R)+(1/L)ln⁡r(λ)−1r(λ)+1G(\lambda) = (1/L) \ln(1/R) + (1/L)\ln\frac{r(\lambda)-1}{r(\lambda)+1}G(λ)=(1/L)ln(1/R)+(1/L)lnr(λ)+1r(λ)−1​

Where r(λ)r(\lambda)r(λ) is the ratio of coherent enhancement and weak light intensity at wavelength λ\lambdaλ in the spontaneous emission spectrum, LLL is the cavity length, and RRR is the reflectivity of the cavity end interface.

If optical gain GGG is measured, we can calculate or simulate α\alphaα and further calculate the spectrum of material gain ggg.
